Upper Canada Village

A gentle day in the 1860s, paced for time with your grandfather

Open in Google Maps

Upper Canada Village is an easy, rewarding choice for a day out together. Walking through the gates feels like stepping into the 1860s, and the whole place moves at a slow, comfortable pace. There is plenty of seated activity and no shortage of shaded spots to rest, which makes it especially kind to older visitors.

The village is open daily 9:30 a.m. to 5:00 p.m. through the main season (May 9 to September 20, 2026), so a 10:00 arrival still gives you a full, unhurried day and keeps the mid-afternoon return ahead of Ottawa rush hour.

01

Easy-paced ways to get around

Miniature train

A scenic, seated ride along the St. Lawrence riverbank, ideal at the start or end of the visit. It is a restful way to take in the park grounds without a step.

Horse-drawn wagons

The full village loop can be tiring on foot. The horse-drawn carryalls move gently through the streets and offer both an authentic experience and a practical way to save energy.

02

Live heritage demonstrations

The bakery

A highlight. Artisans bake bread in a traditional brick oven, and you can often buy a warm loaf of the village's well-known multi-grain bread to take home.

Blacksmith and wheelwright

Tradespeople shape iron and build wooden wheels using 19th-century methods. These shops tend to spark the best conversations about how things used to be made.

Flour and woollen mills

The industrial side of the era. Watching the great wooden gears turn to grind grain and process wool makes the whole place feel genuinely lived-in.

03

Dining and rest stops

Willard's Hotel

Period-style dining with staff in historic dress. The right pick if you want lunch to feel like part of the tour rather than a break from it.

The Harvest Barn

For something quicker and more casual: cafeteria-style comfort food with roomy indoor seating, and an easy place to sit back and recharge.
